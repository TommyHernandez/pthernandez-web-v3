This book comes after "The Black Book of Programmers". In it, Rafael takes us through what we normally call the "software development lifecycle".
In this book, Rafael focuses on the most common practices of clean code, refactoring, design principles, testing and configuration management, and as always adds his own insights about the nature of software. It's a book I liked but by the time I read it I was already very familiar with agile development and clean code principles. So it contributed little to me. Still, I again recommend this book for junior developers, or people who are still studying, since it will guide them towards best practices and you can never have too much of that. As my father used to say, "teach the little tree while it's young".
