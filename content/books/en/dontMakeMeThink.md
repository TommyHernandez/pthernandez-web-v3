In this case, it's a book about usability, not development. In this third edition, Steve has corrected, updated, and generally improved what was already a good book. It should be noted that this book was revised in 2014 and at the pace the industry moves, it could probably be revised again. In this book, Steve talks to us about usability focused on the web for traditional desktop and also the mobile world (applications and web) based on design principles rather than technological principles. Pointing out something obvious many times, the important thing is the content and he gives you tricks to structure that content in a usable way. He dedicates a good part of the book to explaining how to conduct a usability test spending little money, it's a brilliant idea, but to my taste it strays a bit from what I expected and I think he dedicates more time than I would like to this. Steve already has another book called "Rocket Surgery Made Easy: The Do-It-Yourself Guide to Finding and Fixing Usability Problems" where he delves deeply into this topic.
In any case, it's a reading from which you can extract good concepts and ideas, because ultimately what matters doesn't go out of style.
