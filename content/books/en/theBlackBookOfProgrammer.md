title: The Black Book of Programmer
author: Rafael Gómez Blanes
language: English
image: el_libro_negro.jpg
---
This book was a pleasant surprise. The title might suggest that Rafael would tell us dark tricks to become a hacker or to make us millionaires. But not at all. The author takes us through the bad and good habits of programmers. As well as related roles such as architect or manager. Rafael narrates in an entertaining way what things we should do to be better at our work, based on his experience. Some or many of the things he tells are common sense and others you probably already do, but still this is a book that I recommend to everyone. It's likely that in many cases you'll see your company or your boss reflected in his words. It's a reading I did a long time ago, a very long time at this point, but I still have good memories of it and would still recommend it for "junior" developers.
